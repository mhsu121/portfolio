Appointment Scheduling

USER RESEARCH | UX/UI DESIGN
Click here or scroll to view case study  

Overview

  • Background: Online appointment scheduling is a large area of focus for our business, as appointments are one of the main drivers of revenue. Due to the complexity of our online scheduling system, we saw many users dropping off before completing their bookings or booking the wrong appointment type, causing extra work for the clinic staff.

    To resolve this, our team conducted competitive research, internal survey / interviews with clinic staff, and user testing with patients to create a guided scheduling flow that helped make the appointment booking process easier.
  • Duration: June 2020 - August 2020 (3 months)
  • My Role: Ideation, Competitive Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, User Testing, Interface Design
  • Tools:
    Figma
    Userzoom

Research + concept

Competitive analysis

As part of the competive analysis, I wanted to look beyond the typical healthcare systems to see what could be learned from their experiences. I bucketed competitors into 3 categories and documented each of their online scheduling experiences:

  • Other healthcare systems, e.g. Kaiser Permanente, Sutter Health, Banner Health, etc.
  • Modern healthcare tech companies, e.g. One Medical, ZocDoc
  • Non-healthcare industry competitors, e.g. fitness companies like ClassPass, restaurant reservation services like Resy, etc.

User research

Our team conducted a 3 part online scheduling benchmark study, including a competitive benchmark survey, a consumer survey, and a clinic staff survey. We presented these findings to business stakeholders, and followed up with clinic staff interviews. Some of our key findings included:

60%

of users prefer online scheduling methods


40%

are likely to switch healthcare providers if online scheduling is not available

80%

of clinic staff frequently have to reschedule appointments made online by patients

Based on these findings, our team recommended:

  • Creating a guided scheduling flow
  • Improving clarity of the patient and visit information forms
  • Focusing on task accomplishment

Initial design

Wireframes

Using Figma, I created two wireframe options for adding a guided approach to the Online Scheduling product:

  • Option 1 was a contained scheduling widget on the doctor profile page with multiple steps
  • Option 2 launched a new page with step-by-step questions

Option 2

Prototyping and Testing

I created a prototype in Figma using the two wireframes I mocked up and tested them in Userzoom. We received 150 responses for the desktop preference test and 115 responses for the mobile responsive preference test.

In our test, we found that users were able to complete their task of scheduling an appointment 5-6 seconds faster using Option 2 instead of Option 1. Some of the feedback we received:

  • “There was a lot of information on that page which could get quite overwhelming while I’m trying to schedule an appointment”
  • “The scheduling area kind of blended in too much with the rest of the information on the page”
  • “It’s easier not looking at multiple questions at a time.”

High-fidelity mockups

Guided scheduling flow

The finalized design is a step by step process that makes it easy for the user to select the right appointment type, whether they are using a computer or their mobile device. It is fast, easy-to-use, and users can keep track of their progress through the flow with the progress indicator.
